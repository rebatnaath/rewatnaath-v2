Brighter Summer Day is a film that I hold dear to my heart. As someone who is passionate about Asian languages, I find myself drawn to the Mandarin dialogue in this Taiwan film. Hearing the language spoken so fluently in the film brings me a sense of joy and satisfaction.

But it's not just the language that makes this film special to me. The directing style of Edward Yang is truly captivating. Even though the film is over 4 hours long, it never feels like it. The attention to detail in every scene and the way the story unfolds keeps me engaged from start to finish. It's one of those films that make you wish it would never end.

Brighter Summer Day is a film that I highly recommend. It's a true masterpiece that showcases not just the beauty of Taiwan, but also how the Japanese occupation changed Taiwanese culture. The Mandarin dialogue, the directing style and the attention to details all come together to make this film an unforgettable experience. If you're a fan of Asian cinema, this film is a must-watch.



"A Summer's Tale" is a film that holds a special place in my heart. One of the reasons I love this film is because of its visually aesthetic setting. The film is mainly focused and filmed around a beach in the summer, which creates a beautiful and inviting atmosphere that I found myself fully immersed in. The sun, sand, and sea all come together to create a lovely backdrop for the story to unfold.

Another reason I love this film is because of its relatable main character, I found myself connecting with Gaspard on a personal level, as he is a character who is just like me in many ways. He's someone who's trying to find his place in the world and figure out what he wants in life, and I think that's something that many people can relate to.


Lastly, I love this film be cause of Margot, the lead female character. Margot is a pretty, understanding and a caring woman. She is always ready to help Gaspard and is always there for him whenever he needs her. Her presence adds depth and complexity to the story and makes the film even more relatable and enjoyable to watch.
