The majority of Nepals languages are members of the Indo-European (of which the Indo-Aryan branch is spoken by around 82 percent of the people) and Tibeto-Burman linguistic families (spoken by about 17 percent). The Dravidian and Austro-Asiatic language groups make up the majority of the other languages spoken in Nepal.

Official Languages

Nepali is the official language of the federal government of Nepal, but the country's constitution also recognizes the linguistic diversity of the nation by allowing each province to select one or more additional official languages. The Nepali Language Commission, a government agency responsible for promoting and preserving the Nepali language, has recommended 14 official languages for the nation's various provinces. These recommended languages include Nepali as well as other languages spoken as mother tongues in Nepal, such as Maithili, Bhojpuri, Tharu, and more. This recognition and respect for linguistic diversity allows for the use of multiple languages in official settings and promotes cultural diversity and inclusivity in the country.

Language and their Families

The languages spoken in Nepal belong to two main language families: Indo-European and Sino-Tibetan. The Indo-European family includes languages such as Nepali, which is the official language of the country, as well as Maithili, Bhojpuri, and others. These languages are part of the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European family. The Sino-Tibetan family includes languages such as Tibetan, Limbu, and others. These languages are part of the Tibeto-Burman branch of the Sino-Tibetan family.

However, it's important to note that not all of these languages are spoken by a significant number of people. The languages that are at least 1% of the population speaks are the ones mentioned above and some other as well, so it's a representation of the most widely spoken languages in Nepal.

It's also worth noting that the linguistic diversity of Nepal is a reflection of the country's rich cultural heritage and history. The presence of multiple languages in Nepal has played an important role in shaping the country's culture and society. The government of Nepal has recognized this diversity and has taken steps to preserve and promote the use of these languages, which helps to ensure that the country's linguistic heritage is passed on to future generations.
